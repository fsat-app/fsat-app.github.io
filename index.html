About this tool


About the authors

This tool was co-developed by Laleh Hunziker, Sophie Locher, Julien Da Costa and Jasmine Abdulcadir from Geneva University hospitals and University of Geneva.

This visual self-assessment tool aims to improve communication, screening, information access, care, and documentation. It can also be used for prevalence studies.

It features watercolor illustrations by Florine Guenat based on selected photographs from Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting in Children and Adolescents: Illustrated Guide to Diagnose, Assess, Inform and Report.

The watercolors evaluated in a study comparing women’s self-assessment of their FGM/C type with the WHO classification identified through specialist examination.

How to use


For Women and the General Public

This tool is for women and other individuals who wish to learn more about genital anatomy and the different types of FGM/C. The illustrations are intended to:

  • Support understanding of bodily differences
  • Help with personal recognition and naming
  • Make it easier to talk with a healthcare professional, if you choose to do so.

You are not required to share your experience with anyone unless you wish to.

For Healthcare Professionals

This tool can be used during healthcare consultations to support communication with patients. It includes watercolor illustrations and simple anatomical drawings. These images may help individuals recognize and describe their own genital anatomy, including different types of female genital mutilation/cutting (FGM/C), as defined by the World Health Organization (WHO).

With the patient’s informed consent, basic sociodemographic and clinical information may be recorded in an anonymized format for documentation, monitoring, or research, in line with ethical standards and data protection regulations.

Important Notice

This tool is for education and support only. It does not give a medical diagnosis and replace care from a trained healthcare professional. If you have questions, concerns, or health needs, you are encouraged to seek care from a qualified and trusted healthcare provider.
